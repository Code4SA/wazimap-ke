About {{ HURUMAP.name }}

{{ HURUMAP.name }} was birthed from the partnership between Nigerian National Bureau of Statistics (NBS) and Code for Africa (CfA) in a bid to improve end users’ accessibility to NBS Data.

Nigeria’s National Bureau of Statistics (NBS) came into being with the enactment of the Statistics Act 2007 following the merger of the Federal Office of Statistics (FOS) and the National Data Bank (NDB). The merger was to give the new agency a national outlook as the apex statistical agency in the country.

NBS is the authoritative source and custodian of official statistics in Nigeria, and coordinates statistical operations of the National Statistical System comprising all Federal Ministries, Departments and Agencies (MDAs), State Statistical Agencies (SSAs) and a number of other data providers.

Code for Africa and its partners work together to facilitate free access to information, particularly for civil society and citizens.

NBS and Code for Africa are committed to the growth of the civic technology community by ensuring Nigeria’s data is readily accessible and freely available through the Nigeria HURUmap.

What’s the data behind the story?

{{ HURUMAP.name }} provides the general public, particularly infomediaries like journalists and data enthusiasts, a friendly ‘plug & play’ toolkit for locating interactive data visualisations and embedding same in their storytelling. The project's underlying data is quality-checked and solely sourced from the National Bureau of Statistics (NBS).

The project is built on Wazimap, an open source platform by OpenUp and Media Monitoring Africa for making census data more understandable.
